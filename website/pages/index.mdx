BRIDGE (Better Research via Inclusive Design-Driven Guided Experiences) is a movement to 
form a community around inclusive design-driven software development. Our mission is to 
bring UI/UX-first design principles to the Research Software community.

On this website you can learn about:

- core principles of inclusive design and accessibility
- practical tips for incorporating UI/UX practices in your development process, at the start of a project or during it
- existing resources for implementing inclusive and accessible design in your software

We're also building a community of practice - if you'd like to join our mailing list, 
please [fill out this form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfqsdwLgQi_0D9sSBeIUlMb0U533pS1UA52MYdS-buR1aEluw/viewform)

## Why learn these practices?

Good user interface (UI) designs improve the usability and accessibility of software. 
Designing accessible and intuitive UIs is a solved problem in the private sector, 
and there are many resources to draw on. BRIDGE is a call to upskill RSEs in best 
practices for user interface and user experience design (UI/UX) and to apply these 
practices earlier in the research software development process.

One of the main ideas of BRIDGE is around prototyping research software designs with users, 
and using those prototypes to get regular feedback about the user interface that feeds 
into future development. This will help RSEs improve stakeholder engagement and make it 
easier to work with non-experts. It is also a good way to build EDIA 
(equity, diversity, inclusion, and accessibility) into your project.
